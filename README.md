# NyxDrop

> **A community map of naloxone access in Australia.**
> Anonymous. No accounts. Built by peers.

If someone may be overdosing **right now**, call **000** immediately.
Paramedics carry naloxone and are often the fastest help.

---

## What this app is

NyxDrop helps people find places that may provide take-home naloxone across Australia.

It is designed for real-life situations where people may be stressed, short on time, and wary of being judged. The app focuses on what matters most before you walk in:

- whether people recently got naloxone there
- whether barriers were reported (ID requests, Medicare requests, cost, long wait, etc.)
- practical details like directions, contact info, opening hours, and naloxone form

## Why people use NyxDrop

- **Plan ahead** for yourself, your friends, or your community
- **Find nearby options quickly** when time matters
- **Learn from recent community reports**, not just official listings
- **Share your experience anonymously** to help the next person

## How to use it

1. Open the map and search by suburb, address, or place name.
2. Tap pins to view details, recent reports, and access notes.
3. Use filters to avoid places where common barriers were recently flagged.
4. Save trusted spots in **My Places**.
5. After a visit, submit a short anonymous report.

## Two modes

### Plan mode
For calm, everyday planning:
- Full map and filters
- Rich place details
- Save and watch places

### Now mode
For urgent moments:
- Prominent **Call 000** action
- Simplified map focused on likely options
- Quick directions and call actions
- DRSABCD first-aid reminder card

## Privacy first

NyxDrop is built to protect users:

- no public accounts required
- anonymous community reporting
- device-based local key instead of personal identity
- one-tap **Forget this device** option

## Accessibility and language

NyxDrop aims to be community-accessible by default:

- mobile-first map interface
- high-contrast and reduced-motion display options
- multiple language support in progress (community-reviewed)

## Community trust model

Map pins combine four kinds of signal:

- **Recency** (what happened recently)
- **Reliability** (longer-term pattern)
- **Verification** (community/official confidence)
- **Guardian notes** (trusted community partners)

The goal is simple: help people make safer, informed decisions before they arrive.

## Important notes

- NyxDrop supports overdose prevention and access planning, but is **not a substitute for emergency services**.
- In an emergency, always call **000**.
- This project uses respectful, person-first harm-reduction language.

## About naloxone in Australia

Take Home Naloxone (THN) provides access without a prescription in many settings, but real-world access can still vary. NyxDrop exists to close that gap with community knowledge.
